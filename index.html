Our Mission

Born from community.
Built on dignity.

Paz y Dignidad Human Rights International Foundation was created to defend the human rights of migrant families from Central America, Mexico, and Latin America — and to protect human rights defenders throughout the Americas.

We accompany migrants with empathy and commitment, offering integral support without discrimination. Through our network, we promote human rights, emotional well-being, and access to essential resources so every person can rebuild their life with dignity.

Solidaridad Dignidad Paz Respeto Integridad Justicia

"We dream of a more humane society where every migrant in the United States lives with dignity, security, hope, and respect for their human rights."

Founded by immigrants and allies who lived the system firsthand. Our leadership brings together human rights activists, immigration attorneys, and community organizers united by one mission: dignity for every migrant.

Habeas Corpus

The right to
challenge detention.

A writ of habeas corpus is a court order compelling the government to justify why a person is being held. It is one of the oldest and most powerful protections in democratic law — and our primary legal tool against arbitrary ICE detention.

We connect families with immigration attorneys, assist in preparing federal petitions, and support them at every stage of the federal court process.

Step 01
Case Assessment

We review detention circumstances and determine whether a habeas petition is the strongest legal path to freedom.

Step 02
Legal Filing

We connect families with volunteer immigration attorneys and assist in preparing the federal habeas corpus petition.

Step 03
Court Hearing

The government must appear before a federal judge and justify the detention — or release the individual immediately.

"Every person detained by ICE has the right to ask a court: why am I here? We make sure that question gets asked — and answered."

Our Programs

Holistic support
from day one.

Since May 2024, our shelter "Asylum Seeker Mission Firewood UMC" has served migrants and asylum seekers — offering food, housing, legal support, psychosocial therapy, yoga, and English classes. We currently serve 100+ families in partnership with local churches.

⚖️
Migration & Human Rights

Weekly legal sessions with volunteer immigration attorneys. We assist with asylum applications, habeas corpus filings, and rights education for newly arrived families.

🏠
Transitional Housing

Emergency hotel programs in Kent and Everett — 50 rooms each — and transitional subsidies for families awaiting work permits. A bridge to independence.

📚
Education & Opportunity

School enrollment for all children, English classes for adults, technical training, and youth mental health support including tutoring and art programs.

🌱
Cultural Roots

Gastronomy festivals, dance, theatre, and cultural celebrations that keep our communities' roots alive while building belonging in a new home.
